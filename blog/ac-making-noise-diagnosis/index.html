AC Making Noise? Diagnose Every Sound

By Air Control Experts  |  Updated April 2025  |  7 min read

🔊 Quick Answer

AC making noise? Rattling = loose panel or debris (low urgency). Squealing = worn fan bearing (book service). Clicking repeatedly = dead capacitor (book service). Banging = broken fan blade or compressor issue (switch off immediately). Hissing = gas leak (switch off, call now). Screaming from outdoor = compressor failing (switch off, call now). Your AC shouldn't sound like a construction site. While all ACs make some noise (compressor hum, fan sound), new or unusual noises are early warning signs that something is failing. Diagnosing the sound correctly can mean the difference between a ₹500 fix and a ₹15,000 compressor replacement. Here's what every AC noise means.

Urgency guide: LOW = monitor, may fix itself   MEDIUM = book service soon   HIGH = switch off, call now

Indoor Unit Noises

🔊 Soft Gurgling / Refrigerant Flow Sound

LOW

A soft bubbling or flowing liquid sound, especially when the AC starts or stops. This is refrigerant moving through the system — completely normal, especially in newer inverter ACs.

✅ Fix: No action needed.

🔊 Clicking at Startup/Shutdown

LOW

1–3 clicks when turning on or off. This is the relay or PCB control board switching the compressor. Normal on all AC brands.

✅ Fix: No action needed.

🔊 Rattling from Indoor Unit

MEDIUM

Something is loose inside or around the indoor unit. Common causes: loose front panel or screws, debris (leaf, insect, dust clump) caught in the fan, or loose mounting plate. May also be vibration from the wall if the mounting bracket is loose.

⚠️ Fix: Check and tighten the front panel. Look into the air outlet for visible debris. If sound persists, call a technician to open the unit.

🔊 Dripping / Water Sound Inside

MEDIUM

Water dripping sound from the indoor unit that you can also hear or feel (water coming inside the room). The drain pipe is blocked — water is backing up and dripping inside instead of draining out.

⚠️ Fix: Drain pipe flush required. Switch off the AC to prevent water damage to walls and electrics. Call a technician.

🔊 Whistling / High-Pitched Squealing from Indoor Unit

MEDIUM

A high-pitched whistle usually means restricted airflow — the fan is working harder than it should. Cause: severely clogged air filter or blocked return air grille. Can also be a worn fan bearing starting to fail.

⚠️ Fix: Clean the air filter immediately. If sound continues after cleaning, the fan motor bearings may be worn — book a service.

🔊 Loud Banging from Indoor Unit

HIGH

Something has broken loose inside the unit — most commonly the fan blade or a broken fan motor mount. The fan blade hitting the coil or housing can cause coil damage quickly.

🚨 Fix: Switch off immediately. Call a technician. Do not run the AC.

🔊 Hissing from Indoor Unit

HIGH

A persistent hissing sound from the indoor unit that isn't normal refrigerant flow sound. Could indicate a refrigerant gas leak at a joint or the expansion valve, or a refrigerant overcharge. Can also be high pressure in the system.

🚨 Fix: Switch off, ventilate the room, call a technician. Gas leaks get worse over time and damage the compressor.

Outdoor Unit Noises

🔊 Steady Compressor Hum

LOW

A consistent low hum from the outdoor unit is the compressor working normally. Inverter ACs vary this hum as they modulate speed. Completely normal.

✅ Fix: No action needed.

🔊 Vibrating / Rattling Outdoor Unit

MEDIUM

The outdoor unit is vibrating against the bracket, wall, or surface. Causes: missing or worn anti-vibration pads, loose bracket screws, loose fan guard panel. Can also happen if compressor mounts are worn in older units.

⚠️ Fix: Check bracket bolts are tight. Add anti-vibration pads. If unit is 5+ years old, get compressor mounts checked.

🔊 Clicking Sounds from Outdoor Unit (Repeated)

MEDIUM

Repeated clicking (not just at startup) from the outdoor unit usually means the compressor is trying to start but failing — the capacitor is weak or dead. The compressor clicks on, can't sustain, and clicks off repeatedly.

⚠️ Fix: Capacitor replacement. Book a technician. Running on a bad capacitor will eventually burn the compressor windings.

🔊 Loud Banging / Clunking from Outdoor Unit

HIGH

A loud bang or repeated clunking from the outdoor unit means something serious: broken compressor valve, loose compressor mount, or a refrigerant slug (liquid refrigerant entering the compressor). This can cause catastrophic compressor failure.

🚨 Fix: Switch off immediately and call a technician. Do not restart the AC.

🔊 Screaming / High-Pitched Squealing from Outdoor

HIGH

A screaming or loud squealing from the outdoor unit almost always means the compressor is failing — specifically, bearing failure or high pressure buildup (the compressor's internal pressure relief is activating). This is an emergency.

🚨 Fix: Switch off immediately and call a technician. This is the sound of a compressor about to seize.

🔊 Buzzing from Outdoor Unit

MEDIUM

Electrical buzzing (different from mechanical hum) from the outdoor unit. Causes: loose electrical connection, contactor buzzing, capacitor buzzing, or earthing issue. Can also be caused by voltage fluctuations common in Delhi NCR.

⚠️ Fix: Get electrical connections checked. Install a voltage stabiliser if power fluctuations are frequent in your area.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it normal for AC to make noise?
Some noise is normal: a steady compressor hum from the outdoor unit, soft refrigerant flow sounds, and 1–3 clicks at startup/shutdown. What is NOT normal: rattling, banging, squealing, screaming, loud buzzing, repeated clicking, or hissing sounds that weren't there before.
Why is my AC making a rattling noise?
Rattling from the indoor unit usually means: loose front panel/screws, debris in the fan, or loose mounting plate. Rattling from the outdoor unit means: loose bracket, missing anti-vibration pads, loose fan guard, or worn compressor mounts. Most rattling causes are inexpensive to fix.
What does a clicking AC sound mean?
1–3 clicks at startup/shutdown are normal relay sounds. Repeated clicking (especially from the outdoor unit) means the compressor is trying but failing to start — usually a weak or dead capacitor. Get this fixed before the compressor windings burn out.
Why is my AC making a loud banging noise?
Banging from the indoor unit: something has broken loose (fan blade, motor mount). Banging from the outdoor unit: broken compressor valve, loose compressor mount, or liquid refrigerant slug. Switch off immediately and call a technician — these can escalate to major damage quickly.
Can I ignore AC squealing noises?
No. Squealing from the indoor unit usually means worn fan bearings — if ignored, the bearing seizes and you need a fan motor replacement. Squealing or screaming from the outdoor unit means the compressor is failing — if ignored, you will need a compressor replacement (₹8,000–₹18,000).
Why does my new AC make noise?
New ACs making noise is almost always an installation problem: the outdoor unit bracket isn't tight, anti-vibration pads are missing, copper pipes are touching walls (transmitting vibration), or the indoor unit panel isn't properly snapped shut. Have the installer return to check the installation.
