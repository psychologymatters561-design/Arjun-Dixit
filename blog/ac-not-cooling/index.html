Your air conditioner is running — compressor humming, fan spinning — yet the room refuses to cool down. It's one of the most frustrating AC problems, especially during Delhi NCR's brutal summers when temperatures breach 45°C. This guide covers every possible cause of an AC not cooling, from the trivial (dirty filter) to the serious (compressor failure), along with what you can check yourself and when you need a professional AC repair technician.

Before you call a technician: Check your AC remote — confirm it's set to Cool mode (not Fan, Dry, or Auto without a set temperature). Confirm the set temperature is lower than room temperature. This sounds obvious but saves a surprising number of service calls.

Quick Diagnosis: What Does "Not Cooling" Look Like?

AC not cooling problems fall into three buckets:

  • AC runs but blows warm or room-temperature air — usually gas, coil, or compressor issue
  • AC cools initially then gradually loses cooling — often dirty filter, dirty coils, or refrigerant leak
  • AC short-cycles (starts and stops frequently) — thermostat, overheating, or oversized unit
  • AC doesn't reach set temperature even after hours — undersized AC for room, heat load too high, or gas low

The 15 Most Common Causes of AC Not Cooling

1. Dirty or Clogged Air Filter

The #1 cause of reduced AC cooling. A dirty filter chokes airflow, causing the evaporator coil to freeze over (paradoxically making things worse). Fix: Remove and clean the filter under running water. Dry completely before reinstalling. Clean every 3–4 weeks during summer.

2. Low Refrigerant (Gas Leak)

Refrigerant is the substance that actually absorbs heat. If gas has leaked out due to a crack in the pipe or a faulty valve, cooling drops drastically. Signs: ice forming on the indoor unit, hissing sound, AC runs continuously without cooling. Fix: A technician must find the leak, repair it, and recharge with R-32 or R-410A gas. Simply topping up gas without fixing the leak is a waste of money.

3. Dirty Evaporator Coil (Indoor Unit)

The evaporator coil inside the indoor unit absorbs heat from your room air. When coated with dust, it can't transfer heat efficiently. The result: reduced cooling, bad odours, and higher electricity bills. Fix: Deep cleaning or chemical wash by a technician. Recommended annually.

4. Dirty Condenser Coil (Outdoor Unit)

The outdoor unit releases the heat absorbed from your room. If the condenser coil is dirty (dust, debris, plastic bags, leaves), it can't reject heat efficiently. In extreme cases, high-pressure cutout trips the compressor. Fix: Wash the outdoor unit coil with water jet. Ensure nothing is blocking the outdoor unit's airflow (minimum 30cm clearance on all sides).

5. Compressor Not Working Properly

The compressor is the heart of the AC. If it's weak, seized, or has a bad capacitor, refrigerant won't circulate properly and cooling will be poor or zero. You may hear clicking sounds from the outdoor unit or the compressor may not start at all. Fix: Capacitor replacement (cheap, common fix) or compressor replacement (expensive). Diagnosis requires a technician with gauges.

6. Faulty Capacitor

Capacitors start the compressor and fan motors. A weak or failed capacitor means the compressor can't start or runs inefficiently. Common in India due to voltage fluctuations. Signs: outdoor unit fan not spinning, compressor not starting, humming sound from outdoor unit. Fix: Capacitor replacement — relatively affordable at ₹400–₹1,200.

7. Frozen Evaporator Coil

When airflow is restricted (dirty filter, blocked return) or gas is low, the evaporator coil freezes solid. You may see ice on the indoor unit or the insulated copper pipe. A frozen coil blocks all airflow, making cooling drop to zero. Fix: Turn AC to Fan-only mode for 2 hours to defrost. Clean the filter. If freezing recurs, gas level needs checking.

8. PCB (Printed Circuit Board) Failure

Modern inverter ACs are controlled by complex PCBs. A faulty PCB can cause erratic operation, error codes on the display, compressor not receiving correct signals, or the AC not responding to temperature settings. Fix: PCB repair or replacement by a certified technician. Don't delay — secondary damage can occur.

9. Wrong AC Size for Room

An undersized AC will run 24/7 and never reach the set temperature. Rules of thumb for India: 0.8–1 ton for ≤120 sq ft, 1.5 ton for 120–180 sq ft, 2 ton for 180–240 sq ft. High ceilings, west-facing rooms, and glass walls increase heat load. Fix: Properly size the AC or add window treatments to reduce heat ingress.

10. Blocked Outdoor Unit

The outdoor unit needs free airflow to reject heat. If it's installed in an enclosed space, against a wall with no clearance, or covered with debris/a cover, it overheats. Fix: Ensure minimum 30cm clearance. Never run AC with outdoor unit cover on. Clear debris from around the unit.

11. Leaking Air (Room Not Sealed)

If doors/windows are open or have large gaps, the AC loses all its cooled air. The unit works non-stop but the room doesn't cool. Fix: Seal gaps around windows and doors. Use curtains or blinds on sun-facing windows.

12. Thermostat or Temperature Sensor Fault

The temperature sensor (thermistor) tells the AC when the room has reached the set temperature. A faulty sensor may make the AC think the room is already cool, causing it to stop the compressor prematurely. Fix: Technician diagnosis and sensor replacement.

13. Refrigerant Overcharge

Too much gas is as bad as too little. Overcharged refrigerant causes liquid slugging in the compressor, high pressure trips, reduced efficiency, and compressor damage over time. This often happens when gas is topped up carelessly without measuring. Fix: Technician must measure with gauges and release excess refrigerant properly.

14. Inverter PCB / Variable Frequency Drive Issue

Inverter ACs have a variable-speed compressor controlled by an inverter board. If the inverter PCB is faulty, the compressor may run at incorrect speed, causing weak cooling and error codes. More common in areas with frequent power cuts. Fix: Inverter board repair or replacement — requires brand-specific knowledge.

15. Expansion Valve or Capillary Tube Blockage

The expansion valve or capillary tube regulates refrigerant flow into the evaporator coil. A blocked expansion device causes liquid refrigerant to back up, reducing evaporator cooling drastically. Signs: high-side pressure too high, low-side pressure too low on gauges. Fix: Requires a skilled HVAC technician — not a DIY repair.

Quick Self-Check Before Calling a Technician

  1. Confirm AC is set to Cool mode, not Fan/Dry/Auto
  2. Check set temperature is below current room temperature
  3. Clean the air filter (most ACs have easy-access front panel)
  4. Clear any blockage around the outdoor unit
  5. Check if there's ice forming on the indoor unit (if yes, switch to fan-only to defrost)
  6. Look for error codes on the display and note them down
  7. Check the MCB/breaker — AC on its own dedicated circuit?
Pro tip: Check your outdoor unit's fan — it should be spinning when the compressor is on. If the fan isn't spinning, the capacitor is likely dead. This is an easy, inexpensive fix.

When Is AC Not Cooling an Emergency?

Call immediately (don't wait) if you notice:

  • A strong chemical smell or hissing near the indoor/outdoor unit (gas leak — ventilate the room)
  • The outdoor unit is sparking, smoking, or making loud banging noises
  • The MCB is repeatedly tripping when the AC is turned on
  • The indoor unit is dripping water excessively onto walls or electrical fittings
  • Error codes E1, E5, E8, P0, P4 on screen (often indicate refrigerant or compressor problems)

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my AC running but not cooling?
The most common causes are dirty air filter, low refrigerant gas, dirty evaporator or condenser coils, or a failing compressor. Start by checking and cleaning the filter. If cooling is still poor, call a technician to check gas pressure and coil condition.
How do I know if my AC needs gas refilling?
Signs of low refrigerant: AC blows slightly warm air even after long run, ice forming on the indoor or outdoor unit, hissing/bubbling sound from pipes, unusually high electricity bills, and the AC taking very long to cool the room.
Can a dirty filter cause AC to not cool?
Yes. A clogged filter is the #1 cause of reduced cooling. It blocks airflow over the evaporator coil, which can cause the coil to freeze, further blocking airflow. Clean your filter every 2-4 weeks during heavy use.
Why does my AC cool for 10 minutes then stop?
This is called short-cycling. Causes include: oversized AC unit, refrigerant overcharge, frozen evaporator coil, faulty thermostat, or compressor overheating due to dirty condenser. A technician should diagnose this to prevent compressor damage.
What is the cost of AC gas refilling in Delhi NCR?
AC gas refilling in Delhi NCR costs approximately ₹1,200–₹2,500 for R-22 and ₹2,000–₹4,500 for R-32 or R-410A refrigerant, depending on unit tonnage and how much gas is needed. Air Control provides transparent pricing with no hidden charges.
My new AC is not cooling properly. What should I do?
For a new AC not cooling: check that installation was done correctly (copper pipe lengths, flaring, vacuuming), verify the unit is the right size for the room, ensure no gas was lost during installation, and confirm the mode is set to Cool (not Fan or Dry). Poor installation is the #1 cause of new AC cooling problems.
